General Tips for Samurai

Use Gekko from the rear and Kasha from the flank to fulfill positional requirements.

Prioritize Meikyo Shisui charges for Gekko and Kasha rather than Yukikaze .

Apply Higanbana if the target will survive for at least 48 seconds to maximize DoT uptime.

Try to use Kaeshi: Setsugekka within 30 seconds of casting Midare Setsugekka without overwriting its buff.

Use Ikishoten and Ogi Namikiri every 120 seconds, ensuring you don't overcap the Kenki gauge.

Employ Hissatsu: Shinten when your Kenki gauge nears the cap to avoid overflow.

Activate Shoha after executing three Iaijutsu moves.

Rotation Overview

Single Target

  • Opener: Meikyo Shisui → Gekko → Ikishoten → Kasha → Yukikaze → Midare Setsugekka → Hissatsu: Senei → Kaeshi: Setsugekka → Meikyo Shisui → Gekko → Higanbana → Ogi Namikiri → Shoha → Kaeshi: Namikiri .

  • Repeat the Midare Setsugekka sequence as filler when abilities are on cooldown.

Two-Target Rotation

  • Follow the single-target rotation while applying Higanbana to both targets if they will survive for 48 seconds.

  • Utilize Hissatsu: Shinten to manage Kenki gauge overflow.

  • Activate Ikishoten and Ogi Namikiri every 120 seconds.

  • Execute Hissatsu: Guren every 60 seconds for consistent AoE damage.

Three+ Target Rotation

  • Opener: Fuko → Oka , Fuko → Mangetsu , Tenka Goken .

  • Use Meikyo Shisui every 55-60 seconds, prioritizing Gekko and Kasha with 3 targets, and Oka and Mangetsu with 4 or more.

  • Cast Hissatsu: Kyuten to manage Kenki and avoid overcap.

  • Utilize Kaeshi: Goken within 30 seconds of using Tenka Goken without overwriting the buff.

  • Activate Shoha after three Iaijutsu moves.
