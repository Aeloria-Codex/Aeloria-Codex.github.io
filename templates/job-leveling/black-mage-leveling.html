Getting Started

Welcome to the Black Mage guide! This guide will help you master the Black Mage job in Final Fantasy XIV by providing core strategies, useful tips, and a breakdown of spell rotations.

Note: The guide does not cover advanced dungeon, trial, or raid encounters in detail but focuses on building strong fundamentals for Black Mage gameplay as you level up.

The goal is to help you become comfortable with your rotation, understand your job’s core mechanics, and offer advice on gearing and improving performance.

Gear Recommendations

Keeping your gear up-to-date is essential. Without proper gear, your damage contribution will suffer. Ideally, your equipment should match your current level, or be no more than a few levels behind.

If you’re progressing through the Main Scenario Quest (MSQ), you’ll regularly earn gear upgrades and coffers to keep your setup competitive.

For leveling outside the MSQ, you can acquire gear through merchants (using Gil), the Market Board, or by exchanging Tomestones and Poetics earned through roulettes and other activities.

Gear Recommendations by Key Levels:

  • Level 50: Augmented Ironworks Gear (IL 130) – Available from Auriana in Mor Dhona.

  • Level 60: Augmented Shire Gear (IL 270) – Purchase from Hismena in Idyllshire.

  • Level 70: Augmented Scaevan Gear (IL 400) – Buy from Enna in Rhalgr’s Reach.

  • Level 80: Augmented Cryptlurker Gear (IL 530) – Obtain from Aymark in Eulmore.

  • Level 90: Augmented Credendum Gear (IL 660) – Purchase from Cihanti in Radz-at-Han.

  • Level 100: Neo Kingdom Gear (IL 700) – Available from Zircon in Solution Nine.

General Tips for Black Mage

Rotation Mastery: Black Mage heavily focuses on maintaining a seamless flow between your Astral Fire and Umbral Ice phases.

DoT Management: Always refresh your Thunder DoT whenever it expires, ensuring that you're keeping up maximum damage over time.

Positioning: Black Mage has limited mobility, so careful positioning is key. Use Aetherial Manipulation to adjust your placement when necessary.

Ley Lines: Drop Ley Lines in safe spots, as they significantly boost your spellcasting speed. Try to stay inside them for as long as possible.

Triplecast: Use Triplecast during movement-heavy mechanics to keep your damage output high while you’re repositioning.

Managing Enochian: Maintain Enochian by alternating your fire and ice phases effectively.

Rotation Overview

Black Mage’s rotation focuses on swapping between Fire and Ice phases. In Astral Fire, you'll deal massive damage with Fire spells, while Umbral Ice restores your MP for another burst phase.

Single Target Rotation

  • Start with Blizzard III → Blizzard IV to enter Umbral Ice.

  • Switch to Fire III → Fire IV x3, refresh your Astral Fire timer with Paradox, and cast another three Fire IV.

  • End your Astral Fire phase with Despair.

  • Use Xenoglossy as it becomes available to prevent overcapping.

  • Cast Paradox when in Umbral Ice for additional MP recovery and damage.

Multi-Target Rotation

  • Alternate Thunder DoTs on multiple targets to maintain uptime.

  • For AoE, use Blizzard II → Freeze followed by Fire II x3 → Flare x2.

  • Refresh Thunder II as needed and use Foul for both weaving and movement purposes.

Off-Global Cooldowns and Utility

  • Use Transpose to switch between Fire and Ice phases when needed.

  • Manaward can help mitigate incoming damage.

  • Manafont allows you to extend your Fire phase by cutting out an Ice phase.

  • Utilize Ley Lines and reposition them with Retrace if necessary.

  • Use Between the Lines to quickly return to your Ley Lines after movement.

  • Triplecast is ideal for casting your most powerful spells instantly, especially during movement.

  • Make use of Amplifier to generate a Polyglot stack for Foul or Xenoglossy.
